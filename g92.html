What is Magnetism?

Magnetism is the force exerted by magnets through a magnetic field when they repel or attract each other. Magnets can attract materials such as iron and steel, which are categorised as magnetic materials. However, it cannot attract materials that are non-magnetic materials such as plastic and rubber.

There are two types of magnetic materials, hard and soft, magnetically hard materials such as steel won't lose their magnetism, once magnetised and therefore can become permanent magnets. On the other hand, magnetically soft materials such as iron lose their magnetism quickly and suit as a temporary magnet.



Poles

In magnetism, there are 2 poles, north and south pole, their positions are the opposite of the geographic earth poles. Poles on a magnet are the strongest parts, which is evident through the magnetic fields.

When the south and north pole get close to each other, they start to attract. However, when the north pole and another north pole tries to get closer, they repel eachother.

Demagnetization

Demagnetization is the action that is done when removing magnetism from an object, this can be done by burning the magnet, hitting the magnet, as well as using alternative currents.


Right-Hand Rule

In electromagnetism, the current in the wire creates a magnetic field around it, hence the circular magnetic field. To find the direction of the magnetic field, we can use the right hand rule. The thumb acts as the direction of the current in the wire, and the curled fingers act as the direction of the magnetic field around the current.








Another application of the right hand rule is when charged particles (proton and electrons) enter the magnetic field. Using our thumb as the direction the charged particle was going to go before being redirected by the magnetic field (keep in mind that the electron is the opposite direction of the proton.) Our fingers act as the direction of the magnetic field entering or leaving the screen. Our palm acts as the force on the charged particles and where they are redirected.


Left-Hand Rule

When a current-carrying conductor is placed in an external magnetic field, the conductor experiences a force perpendicular to both the field and the current flow's direction
