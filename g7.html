Kinematics

"Velocity without direction be like...I AM SPEED." - Mr. Welly




Speed:

Speed is the rate at which an object moves over a distance in a given amount of time.

It can be calculated by dividing the distance an object traveled with the time it took to cover that distance.

Formula:
Speed = Distance/Time
Time = Distance/Speed
Distance = Speed x Time

Velocity:

Velocity is the speed in combination with the direction of the motion of an object.

It can be calculated by dividing the displacement between two points with the time it took to cover the displacement.

Formula:
Velocity = Displacement/Time
Time = Displacement/Velocity
Displacements = Velocity x Time



Acceleration:

Acceleration is the rate of change in velocity with respect of time.

It can be calculated by dividing the amount of velocity that changes within the unit of time.

Formula:
Acceleration = Change in Velocity/Change in Time
OR Acceleration = Final Velocity - Initial Velocity/Time
